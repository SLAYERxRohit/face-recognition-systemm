The Viesta Pipeline

Viesta executes a rigorous computer vision and machine learning pipeline to process uploaded images and identify characteristics. Here is the step-by-step breakdown of what happens behind the scenes:

  • 1
    Pre-processing & Face Detection

    The raw image is loaded and converted to grayscale. An OpenCV Haar Cascade Classifier scans the image to identify facial structures and crops the region of interest (ROI).

  • 2
    Normalization & Alignment

    The cropped face is normalized (pixel values divided by 255.0) and resized to a consistent 100x100 grid, preparing it for mathematical analysis.

  • 3
    PCA Feature Projection

    The 10,000-pixel vector is centered against a mean face. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) maps the centered face onto the top eigenvectors, creating a compressed representation known as an Eigenface.

  • 4
    SVM Classification

    The PCA coordinate vector is processed by a Support Vector Machine (SVM) model. SVM maps the coordinate onto a hyper-plane, calculating the gender classification boundaries.
